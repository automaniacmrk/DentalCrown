Information about Dental Crowns

A dental crown is a useful dental treatment that can extend the life of your natural tooth. It will help strengthen the tooth and also protect it from further damage. Most of the dental crowns that Dr. Tu places are made of porcelain because they resemble natural teeth and are highly durable. A dental crown is a tooth-shaped "cap" that is cemented to a natural tooth. It will completely encase the tooth down to the gum line. The top reasons Dr. Tu utilizes tooth crowns include:

  • Cosmetic enhancement
  • After a tooth receives a root canal
  • When a tooth is fractured or broken
  • Severe decay
  • When a dental filling is cracked

Inlays And Onlays

Also known as partial crowns, inlays and onlays are custom-made restorations for patients with worn, damaged teeth. Inlays are used to cover the chewing surfaces between the cusps or sides of the teeth. The entire tooth surface, including its cusps, is covered by onlays.

Both inlays and onlays are made of composite resin or porcelain material. Inlays and onlays are able to blend with your natural smile. Like tooth crowns, inlays and onlays are attached to a prepared tooth. These are made to match the shape of your tooth. For long-lasting restorations, inlays and onlays can be attached to the teeth with resin.

The Best Dentist Crown Material

Each of the materials has its own set of advantages and disadvantages, so the right crown material for you will depend on which teeth are being capped, how you want them to look esthetically, and how long you need them to last.

Ceramic crowns can be made in a laboratory and are built layer by layer using CAD/CAM technology. Ceramic crowns have a translucent look similar to natural teeth, making them popular for front teeth. You can even create them chairside with a new technology that mills crowns in a matter of minutes. They are easy to complete in one visit, and don't require a provisional cap.

Ceramic materials can be weaker than metal, and are more susceptible to cracking or breaking over time. However, if fabricated properly, ceramics should last for at least 15 years. Some ceramic crowns, like Emax caps, are designed differently to be more durable.

Gold dental crowns and those made from other alloy metals tend to be much more durable. Biocompatible means that the crowns can be used with any teeth. Caps are not likely to cause damage or wear. If the patient takes care of it, a gold crown will last for a long time.

Any metal crown has one major drawback: the color. A lot of people don't like a silver or gold-colored crown on their teeth, no matter how it may be hidden at the back.

Others find a crown of gold very appealing. They may even go so far as to have the crown engraved with or studded in gemstones.

Tooth cap prices can vary from one clinic to the other, as with all dental treatments. If you are looking to crown more than one tooth, it is worth shopping around.

Also, ask about other options. It is a good idea to inquire about the options available to you from your dentist if you find yourself in similar circumstances.

Full ceramic crowns, on the other hand, are more expensive than full metal ones. Front tooth crowns can be more costly as more time needs to be put into giving them a natural look.

Receiving A Dental Crown In Detail Dental

Having a dental crown placed will require at least two visits to Detail Dental area dentist office. During the first visit, Dr. Tu will prepare the tooth. He will first administer local anesthetic. Then he will remove all old fillings, debris, and the decayed portion of the tooth. The tooth will need to be shaped so that the crown fits properly. Next, he will take several impressions of your teeth that will be used to fabricate the tooth cap. Finally, Dr. Tu will attach a temporary crown to your tooth. This is necessary in order to preserve the tooth while the permanent crown is made.

It will take around two weeks to receive the permanent dental crown from the dental laboratory. We will then schedule the second appointment to place your cap. First, Dr. Tu will remove the temporary crown and thoroughly clean the tooth. Then the new cap will be cemented onto your tooth for a permanent fit. We will send you home with instructions on how to care for your new tooth crown.

Materials for Dental Crowns

There are various materials that can be used to construct a dental crown, including:

  • Precious metal-bonded porcelain: the most common material used for crowns. The base is made of precious metal for increased strength and porcelain is layered over it for improve aesthetics.
  • Porcelain is less durable than bonded porcelain, but it provides an extremely natural appearance for aesthetic purposes such as to cap front teeth.
  • Ceramic is a flexible metal-free material which can look similar to porcelain.
  • Glass can be used anywhere on the lips for an extremely natural appearance.
  • The gold-alloy is a classic material, which is strong and can be used in many applications. It is also available in silver or golden color.

How do Dental Crowns Work?

  • Ceramic: Often used to restore the front teeth because of their natural color, ceramic crowns are made of a porcelain-based material.
  • Resin: Most often used for a temporary crown, resin is the least-durable option. Resin may have a short lifetime due to its tendency to fracture.
  • Porcelain fused to metal: This is a stronger option than regular porcelain, as it is fused to the metal structure; it is highly durable.
  • Base metal alloys: Very durable crown can be made of non-noble metals that are corrosion-resistant.
  • Gold alloys: Gold bonds strongly to the tooth and is unlikely to fracture; these crowns are made of a mixture of gold, copper, and other metals.

Why Dental Crowns Are Used

There are many uses for dental crowns, including:

  • Protect a weak tooth. Teeth can decay, break, crack, and chip. To keep the tooth together, a cap is placed over it. A protected tooth means a healthier smile!
  • To restore a broken tooth. Caps can also be used to restore a tooth that is severely worn down.
  • Improve your smile. You can instantly improve your smile by covering any discolored or damaged tooth.
  • To provide support for a dental bridge. A dental bridge requires two healthy teeth on either side. If the bride needs further support, a dental crown may be needed.

Looking to improve the appearance or healthiness of your smile? A dental cap could be the right solution for you!

Crown Application Process

In order to get the crown, your dentist will usually need to visit you twice. During the first visit, your dentist will file down your tooth and shape it in preparation for the crown. A temporary crown is fitted to you for the first visit. The permanent crown will then be placed at the second appointment.

When you arrive for your first appointment, your dentist will start by numbing your mouth with some local anesthetic. You'll then be asked to bite onto some carbon paper to see how the crown needs to be placed in order to not interfere with your bite. Then, your dentist will insert a dental dam in your mouth before using a drill to reshape your tooth. This process will cause vibrations, but not pain. The impression of the tooth will then be taken and sent to a laboratory. The temporary crown will be made by your dentist using a special material. This temporary crown can then be worn until the next visit.

You'll need to take a few precautions when wearing a temporary crown:

  • Try the other side.
  • Avoid sweetened foods such as caramel and taffy.
  • Instead of lifting the crown, floss around the tooth and not lift it.

When you return to have the permanent crown attached, your dentist will numb your mouth once again, remove the temporary crown, and then use cement to attach the permanent crown.

A same-day porcelain crown can be made using CAD/CAM technology. This will save you the hassle of having to use temporary crowns. It should only take your dentist about 20 minutes to make the crown.

After your permanent crown has been placed, you will be able to care for it as you would any natural tooth. Brush at least twice a day, and floss daily as recommended by your dentist. Regular checkups with your dentist are recommended so that they can monitor the crown and spot any issues early.

Contact your dentist if you have any symptoms after a crown is placed.

  • Pain when chewing
  • Pain in your jaw or head
  • Crowns with a loose, wiggly feel
  • Jagged or rough spots on the crown

It's normal for the crowned tooth to feel a bit sensitive to heat and cold after treatment. If the sensitivity persists for more than a week, contact your dentist.

Do Dental Crowns Require Special Care?

A dental crown is not a guarantee for full protection of your tooth, so you still need to care for it as though it were another tooth. Good oral hygiene means brushing twice daily, flossing once per day and using antibacterial mouthwash to rinse it off.

The Bite Problem

To ensure that a crown fits well and feels comfortable, our dentist takes images and impressions of each patient before fitting it. However, if your crown doesn't seem to fit quite right or feels abrasive when you bite down, then it's important to schedule an exam.

Malocclusions can cause excessive wear to neighboring teeth. Contact us immediately if your crown is not fitting properly. This type of issue can be easily avoided with a good fit, but it can also be resolved if it occurs.

Crowns Are Meant To Last

If you take care of your crowns, they can last a lifetime. Some crowns can be more susceptible to breaking or chipping than others, but it is up to you how to care for them.

Taking proper care of your dental crown involves:

  • Visiting your dentist for regular checkups and cleanings
  • Brushing and flossing at least twice a day
  • Hard foods like hard candy and ice should be avoided
  • Sticky foods like caramel and toffee should be avoided.
