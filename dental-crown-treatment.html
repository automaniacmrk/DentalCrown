Dental Crown Treatment

Dr. Bellisario ensures that all patients are suitable candidates for dental crowns before they begin treatment. If needed, he will recommend dental extractions, periodontal care, or root canals to ensure that patients are in good oral health. For patients who need a tooth crown to cap off a dental implant, Dr. Bellisario makes sure that they have enough jaw bone tissue to support their implant. Then, he will begin their dental crown treatment.

First, Dr. Bellisario will prepare the tooth for the crown. He removes a small portion of the enamel to make sure that the tooth crowns fit properly. Then, he takes dental impressions. The impressions are then sent to the dental laboratory where they are fabricated to exact specifications. Once the dental crowns are complete, Dr. Bellisario attaches the crowns to the teeth. After a dental implant procedure, patients who need crowns will have to wait until their implants heal to receive their crowns.

Dental crowns are durable if taken care of properly. Keep your teeth clean and flossing daily to care for dental crowns. By taking care of your gums and natural teeth, you can reduce the risk of tooth decay and gum disease. We can replace any damaged or old dental crowns. We can help you create your custom restoration.

All-Ceramic

All-ceramic crowns are made from zirconia and aluminous. They provide a metal-free esthetic option with a number of benefits.

An all-ceramic crown with reduced material thickness can be made by eliminating the requirement for a supportive metal core. This makes it a more favorable treatment choice in areas with limited space. Additionally, the elimination of the metal core allows for light transmission through the porcelain for better optical, life-like properties and a higher level of aesthetics.

While all-ceramic materials are advancing in strength and endurance, it is important to be cautious about areas that require heavy functionality. Continuing research is exploring the significant vulnerabilities of the porcelain systems in such areas.

Recovery

Dental crowns, also known as "caps," preserve the functionality of damaged teeth. Common dental restorations can be used to repair or restore function of damaged teeth, replace an existing crown, and protect cracked teeth. This custom-designed tooth restoration covers a damaged tooth. Dentists today have a variety of conservative treatment options through which to restore teeth. These options should all be discussed and explored before deciding on a full-coverage crown.
